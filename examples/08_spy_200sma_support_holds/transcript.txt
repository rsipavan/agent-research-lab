Does the 200-day moving average actually work as support on SPY?

I've been trading for a while and you hear this all the time: "price respects the 200-day moving average." But does it actually? I wanted to find out, so I went back through five years of SPY data and tested it properly.

Here's the setup. I defined a test as: SPY daily close is within 1% above the 200-day simple moving average. That's the zone where you'd expect buyers to step in if the rule works. Then I measured: what percentage of those test events resulted in SPY being higher 10 trading days later?

The answer: out of 52 occurrences from January 2019 through December 2023, 38 of them — 73% — resulted in SPY closing higher 10 trading days after the signal.

Now this isn't a perfect rule and I want to be clear about the caveats. The 2019-2023 window includes a long bull market, which obviously creates upward bias in any "bounce from support" test. And the 200 SMA held as support during most of this period, so any downside breach would have invalidated the test anyway.

But the point stands: when SPY is trading in the zone just above the 200-day, it has historically been a higher-probability area to be a buyer on the daily chart. 73% of 52 occurrences is statistically meaningful and well above what you'd expect from random chance.

The specific setup: SPY daily, close within 1% above the 200-day SMA, measured over a 5-year lookback from January 2019 to December 2023. 10-day forward return. That's a concrete, checkable claim and it holds up.
